# Style guide — `nature/` sub-corpus

Voice: natural-history / evolutionary biology. Read like a Nature News piece or a chapter on translational biology.

## Anchoring

- Cite peer-reviewed biology / ecology / evolution sources for the natural-history claim, and a clinical / pharmacology source for the medical-relevance claim.
- Be specific about taxa (Latin name) and geography.

## Claim discipline

- **Every claim ends with a `[N]` footnote.**
- Distinguish "this organism inspired drug X" from "this organism produces compound Y that is drug X" — they're often confused.
- Avoid teleology ("evolved in order to") — use "evolved such that" or describe selection pressure.

## Section ordering (required)

1. `## Summary` (organism, the natural product or phenomenon, the medical relevance)
2. `## Natural history` (how the organism / system actually works)
3. `## Medical translation` (how this informs human therapeutics — heavy citation)
4. `## Open questions / ongoing research`
5. `## See also`
6. `## Sources`

## Cross-link guidance

`clinical/` pages may pull a `nature/` page as `optional_deep_dive` framed as `natural_history` — e.g. horseshoe crab LAL after a sepsis / endotoxin case. **Never** as primary evidence for management.
