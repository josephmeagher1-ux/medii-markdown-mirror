# Style guide — `history/` sub-corpus

Voice: narrative, dated, biographical. Read like a Wellcome Collection short essay rather than a clinical guideline.

## Anchoring

- Cite primary sources where possible (the original publication, biographical archive, Wellcome catalogue entry).
- Distinguish what the historical figure **demonstrated** from what is **attributed** to them later. Many eponyms attach to the wrong person.
- Include dates (year, ideally the specific publication) for every milestone.

## Claim discipline

- **Every claim ends with a `[N]` footnote.** Same rule as clinical.
- Be explicit when modern reinterpretation differs from the original claim ("Corrigan described the pulse in 1832; the modern term *Corrigan's pulse* was popularised by ...").
- Do not present historical understanding as current clinical guidance. The `framing: history_eponym` tag in trivia cards exists precisely to keep these separate.

## Section ordering (required)

1. `## Summary` (one paragraph: who, when, what)
2. `## Timeline` (key dated events)
3. `## Modern relevance` (how the historical work informs current practice — heavily caveated)
4. `## Misattributions / controversies`
5. `## See also`
6. `## Sources`

## Cross-link guidance

When linking to a `clinical/` page, the link is **historical context only**. The clinical page treats it as `optional_deep_dive` material, never as authoritative for management.
