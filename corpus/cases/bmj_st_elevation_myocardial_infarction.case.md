# ECG diagnosis of STEMI and its mimics

**Difficulty:** medium  •  **Tags:** STEMI, ECG, posterior MI, left main obstruction, LBBB  •  **Estimated time:** 15 min

## Stem
A 62-year-old man presents to the Emergency Department with central crushing chest pain radiating to the left arm, ongoing for 45 minutes. He is diaphoretic and nauseated. Initial observations show heart rate 104 bpm, BP 88/56 mmHg. An ECG is performed at triage.

## Information available
- ECG showing ST-segment changes
- Vital signs as above
- Clinical history of chest pain and haemodynamic compromise

## Questions
1. **MCQ** What is the minimum ST-segment elevation required in leads V2-V3 to diagnose STEMI in this patient?
   A. ≥1 mm
   B. ≥1.5 mm
   C. ≥2 mm
   D. ≥2.5 mm
   E. ≥3 mm

2. **MCQ** The ECG shows deep ST depression in leads V1-V3. What should you suspect?
   A. Anterior STEMI
   B. Posterior STEMI
   C. Left main coronary artery obstruction
   D. Pericarditis
   E. Normal variant

3. **MCQ** In a patient with left bundle branch block (LBBB), which ECG finding best indicates STEMI?
   A. New LBBB alone
   B. Discordant ST elevation
   C. Concordant ST-segment elevation
   D. ST depression in V1-V3
   E. T-wave inversion

4. **SAQ** Describe the ECG pattern that suggests complete left main coronary artery obstruction, especially in a shocked patient.

## Model answers
1. **C. ≥2 mm** – For men >40 years old, the cut-off in V2-V3 is ≥2 mm; using 1 mm would be incorrect.
2. **B. Posterior STEMI** – Deep ST depression in V1-V3 is a key indicator of posterior STEMI, not anterior STEMI.
3. **C. Concordant ST-segment elevation** – Concordant ST elevation (in leads with positive QRS) is a strong indicator; new LBBB alone does not diagnose STEMI.
4. The pattern is ST depression ≥1 mm in ≥6 surface leads (inferolateral ST depression) plus ST elevation in aVR or lead V1. This should raise suspicion for left main obstruction, especially with haemodynamic compromise.

## Rubric
**Must mention:**
- ≥2 mm ST elevation in V2-V3 for men >40 years
- ≥1 mm ST elevation in other contiguous leads
- Posterior STEMI indicated by deep ST depression in V1-V3
- Left main obstruction pattern: ST depression ≥1 mm in ≥6 leads plus ST elevation in aVR or V1
- Concordant ST elevation in LBBB
- New LBBB alone does not diagnose STEMI

**Must not say:**
- Using 1 mm ST elevation cut-off in V2-V3 for a 62-year-old man
- Diagnosing STEMI solely on the basis of new LBBB without concordant ST changes
- Missing posterior STEMI by ignoring ST depression in V1-V3
- Failing to consider left main obstruction in a shocked patient with widespread ST depression and aVR elevation
